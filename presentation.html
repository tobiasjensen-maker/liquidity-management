✅ The promise

Every use case addresses a real pain point. Business owners want to see what's coming. Accountants want advisory tools. The data already exists in e-conomic — invoices, payment patterns, recurring expenses, bank balances.

⚠️ The reality

The customer still sees only historical data. No one has validated whether users will trust and act on AI recommendations — not just view them. The riskiest assumption isn't technical feasibility; it's behavioural adoption.

Experiment timeline — reducing uncertainty before building
Three critical hypotheses across desirability, feasibility, and viability — each with lean tests designed to derisk the investment.
Week 1–2
Desirability
Prototype usability test
Show interactive prototype to 8–10 business owners. Observe: do they understand the forecast? Do they click recommendations?
Success threshold
>40% click a recommendation
Week 1
Desirability
Fake door test
Add ‘Likviditetsoverblik’ link in Rapporter sidebar. Measure click-through and beta sign-up rate.
Success threshold
>5% of visitors click
Week 2–4
Feasibility
Backtest historical data
Pull 12 months of invoice + bank data for 50 anonymized SME accounts. Compare predicted vs. actual at 30/60/90 days.
Success threshold
Within 15% for 70% of accounts
Week 1–2
Viability
Willingness-to-pay survey
Survey 200 users with prototype screenshots. Test 3 price points for predictive cash flow alerts.
Success threshold
>25% would pay DKK 99/mo
What we don't know yet
Honest uncertainty is a feature of this vision, not a weakness.
User trust in AI

Will users trust and act on AI-generated recommendations?

Mitigation: Prototype usability testing with real BOs
Forecast accuracy

Can we predict within 15% using existing data alone?

Mitigation: Backtest on 50 anonymized accounts
Bank integration

MitID consent flow may require 12+ month partnerships.

Mitigation: Scope V1 without bank integration
Revenue vs. retention

Premium upsell or retention play? Wrong framing changes everything.

Mitigation: WTP survey + beta cohort tracking
